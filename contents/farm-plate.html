Farm Plate

If you think Daraga, Albay is only about historic ruins and the iconic Mayon Volcano, think again. This vibrant town is not only rich in heritage—with churches, colonial structures, and landscapes that echo centuries of Filipino history—but also brimming with modern attractions that reflect its growth, creativity, and resilience. And nestled quietly in its scenic hillsides lies one of the most delightful surprises in Bicol: FarmPlate.

The Happiest Place in Bicol

Yes, that’s what many call it—and for good reason. FarmPlate is a farm-to-table ecotourism destination just a few minutes from Daraga town proper. With panoramic views of Mayon, a peaceful countryside vibe, and a charming cowboy-themed atmosphere, this place offers a refreshing blend of nature, leisure, and modern flair.

It’s not just a farm—it’s an experience. Whether you're traveling with family, a group of friends, or your special someone, FarmPlate promises moments that feel both nostalgic and new.

Lights, Camera, Relaxation!

You may have seen glimpses of FarmPlate in the film *I Love Lizzy*. It’s no surprise the filmmakers chose this location: it’s cinematic in every sense. Think rolling hills, rustic barns, glowing tunnels of light, and quiet corners perfect for reflection or photography. And did we mention it’s home to the smallest chapel in the Philippines? Perched on a hill, this tiny church offers spiritual serenity and one of the best views in town.

Things you can do at Farm Plate

Whether day or night, FarmPlate has something for everyone. Explore the “love tunnel” lit with glowing hearts. Get up close with farm animals like sheep and cows. Enjoy bonfire nights with live acoustic music. Let kids play freely in well-maintained playgrounds. Join ATV rides, biking, horse grazing, or chill with a carabao ride. Relax in tents, hammocks, or cottages. Savor local food and Bicolano delicacies. Capture Instagram-worthy shots against lush scenery. y evening, the farm transforms into a glowing spectacle of lights, perfect for romantic dates or peaceful reflection under the stars.
Farm Plate, Gabawan, Daraga, Albay

Food, Culture & Cowboy Vibes

FarmPlate doesn’t just focus on fun—it’s a tribute to Bicolano culture and food, too. From local produce to hearty meals, every detail is designed to honor the flavors and traditions of the region. The cowboy-inspired decor adds a unique twist that sets it apart from other farm destinations. It’s this fusion of rural charm and contemporary comfort that makes FarmPlate a modern wonder of Daraga.

Planning Your Visit!

Location: Farm Plate, Gabawan, Daraga, Albay Transport: Either at least PHP 50 tricycle ride or via private vehicle, from Daraga town proper Entrance Fee: PHP 150 per head Best Time to Visit: Late afternoon to evening for full light display

Why You Should Go!

FarmPlate is more than a photo-op spot—it’s a retreat, a community hub, a family park, and a cultural showcase rolled into one. Whether you’re into adventure, music, relaxation, or local food, there’s something here for you. So if you’re planning a trip to Albay, don’t just stop at the ruins. Head a little farther into the hills and let FarmPlate show you what modern Daraga is all about: resilient, rooted, and ready to welcome you with open arms and open skies.

Mount Mayon

Daraga, Albay

Mayon Volcano, located in the province of Albay in the Bicol Region of the Philippines, is renowned for its near-perfect symmetrical cone shape. Standing at 2,463 meters (8,081 feet), it is one of the most active volcanoes in the country and a prominent symbol of natural beauty and danger. Its frequent eruptions have shaped both the landscape and the lives of the people living around it, making it a vital part of Bicolano culture and identity.

Daraga Church

Sta. Maria Street, Daraga, Albay

One of the few of Albay’s heritage churches that withstood the effects of Mayon volcano’s eruptions is the Daraga Church or also known as Nuestra Señora de la Porteria Parish Church. Perched on a hill with a commanding view of the majestic Mayon Volcano, Daraga Church stands as a lasting testament of faith, artistry, and resilience of Albayanos.

Budiao Church Ruins

Daraga, Albay

The first settlers of the town of Daraga came from a place called Budiao. Under the leadership of Juan Salcedo, the Spaniards arrived and established this village. The name "Budiao" is derived from the word "badjao," which means "the greatest," as demonstrated by the "ilustrados" (educated elite) among the spectators of bullfights. In Budiao, there was a designated area for the battle between men and bulls, following a tradition reminiscent of ancient Rome.

Cagsawa Ruins

Barangay Busay, Daraga, Albay

The Cagsawa Ruins (also spelled as Kagsawa, historically spelled as Cagsaua) are the remnants of a 16th-century Franciscan church, the Cagsawa church. It was originally built in the town of Cagsawa in 1587 but was burned down and destroyed by Dutch pirates in 1636. It was rebuilt in 1724 by Fr. Francisco Blanco but was destroyed again, along with the town of Cagsawa, on February 1, 1814, during the eruption of Mayon Volcano.
